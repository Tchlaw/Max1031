## üß† What Is a 1031 Exchange?

A **1031 exchange** ‚Äî named after Section 1031 of the Internal Revenue Code ‚Äî allows you to sell one **investment or business property** and buy another *like-kind property* **while deferring capital gains tax** that would normally be due at sale.

This gives investors the ability to:

- Preserve equity rather than lose it to taxes
- Reposition into stronger or more strategic assets
- Compound long-term wealth without immediate tax erosion

---

## üß© Key Requirements

To qualify for a 1031 exchange, you must meet **all** of the following:

- Property must be held for **investment or business use**
- You must **identify replacement property within 45 days**
- You must **close on the replacement within 180 days**  
  (or by your tax filing due date, if earlier)
- A **Qualified Intermediary (QI)** must hold the funds ‚Äî you can‚Äôt receive or control the proceeds
- The same taxpayer/entity that sells must be the one that buys

## üîÅ How a 1031 Exchange Works (Step-by-Step)

1. **Plan early** ‚Äî speak with your CPA, legal advisor, and QI  
2. **Sell your property** ‚Äî sale proceeds go directly to the QI  
3. **Identify replacements** ‚Äî submit your **45-day written list** to your QI  
4. **Perform due diligence** ‚Äî inspect, negotiate, line up financing  
5. **Close on the replacement** ‚Äî must be completed within **180 days**  
6. **Report the exchange** ‚Äî file IRS **Form 8824** with your tax return  

> üí° **Timeline Tip:**  
> Day 1 starts the *day after* your sale closes. The 45- and 180-day deadlines are strict ‚Äî extensions almost never apply.

## üè∑Ô∏è The 45-Day Identification Rules

There are three IRS-approved methods to identify replacement properties:

- **3-Property Rule:** Identify up to **3 properties of any value**
- **200% Rule:** Identify any number of properties, **total value ‚â§ 200%** of what you sold
- **95% Rule:** Identify any number of properties, but you must **close on at least 95%** of the total value identified

## üí∏ Avoiding Boot: Replacing Equity & Debt

To **fully defer taxes**, you must:

- Buy property of **equal or greater value**
- Reinvest **all net equity** (sale proceeds)
- Replace **equal or greater debt**  
  (cash can be added to offset a debt shortfall)

If you take cash out or reduce debt without replacing it, the difference is considered **boot**, which is typically taxable.
